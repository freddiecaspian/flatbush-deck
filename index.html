Mechanism

  1. Lintner violation - no raise despite sustainable new earnings = management doesn't believe guidance

WHY FIXED-PRICE TENDER? — VERMAELEN (1981) PERMANENT ABNORMAL RETURN
Tender commits capital publicly, sets a floor price, all-or-nothing. Vermaelen's sample covers 1962-1977; self-tenders were legally available in 1959 but less standardised (pre-Williams Act 1968). An open-market programme or formal tender-style repurchase is the viable 1959 mechanism; economics hold either way.
One move. Returns ~$6m, captures tax shields, re-levers the balance sheet, sends the signal Davis was trying to manufacture - but real, not theatrical.
