Why did the stock price fall when the company announced its dividend policy?
The market priced the stock below even Davis's gloomy case
$78 → $69
-11.5% in one week. The market was pricing future cuts, not just a flat dividend.
Yield basis: 1958 average dividend yield of 6.2% (Exhibit 3). Padgett's $14.50 EPS guidance × 50% payout rule → $7.25 dividend. Davis's flat dividend → $4.80. Anchors = dividend ÷ 6.2%.
The market didn't just price in Davis's world — it priced in something worse. Holding the dividend flat at a moment of rising earnings told investors management didn't believe its own forecast, so they priced in future cuts. The $69 price reflects that pessimism.
