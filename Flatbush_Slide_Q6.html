WHY ZERO DEBT IS WRONG

Tax shield lostDebt interest is tax-deductible. At 52% corporate tax, every $1 of interest saves 52¢. Flatbush captures none of this.
Idle cash problemWithout debt service to fund, management sits on $7.28m. Debt forces distribution (Jensen agency cost, S09).
Backlog supports it$190m of signed orders = 2.5 years of visibility. Interest coverage would be ~23× — no distress risk.

Plain English: Issue $4m of long-term debt. Combine with $2m of surplus cash. Use the $6m to buy back about 20% of the shares. Result: Flatbush finally captures the tax benefits of debt, stops sitting on idle cash, and returns capital to shareholders — all with interest coverage so high (~23×) that distress risk is negligible.
Trade-off theory (S08-S09): the optimal capital structure balances tax shields, distress costs, and agency costs. Flatbush sits at a corner — zero debt — that only makes sense if all three forces pulled the same direction. They don't.
